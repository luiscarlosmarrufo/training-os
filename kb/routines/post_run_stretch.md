# Post-Run Stretching Routine

## Overview
Comprehensive stretching routine to promote recovery and maintain flexibility after running. Hold static stretches for specified duration.

## Quick Version (5 minutes)
1. **Seated quad stretch** - Sit on butt, lean back
2. **Hamstring stretch** - Grab toes, straight legs
3. **Seated pigeon** - Glute stretch
4. **Yoga cobra flow** - Dynamic spine mobility

## Full Version (15 minutes)

### Lower Body Stretches
1. **Deep lunge stretch** - 1 minute each leg
   - Back knee down, sink into hip flexor

2. **World's greatest stretch** - 10 reps each side
   - Lunge position with rotation

3. **Child's pose to cobra flow** - 10 reps
   - Full spine mobility

### Posterior Chain
4. **Downward dog** - 1 minute
   - Primary calf stretch
   - Pedal feet for dynamic stretch

5. **Pigeon stretch** - 1 minute each side
   - Deep glute and hip stretch

6. **Heel sit** - 2 minutes
   - Quad and ankle stretch

7. **Hamstring stretch** - 1 minute each leg
   - Lying or standing version

## Alternative Quick Routine
1. **Quad stretch** - 30 sec each
2. **Hamstring stretch** - 30 sec each
3. **Glute stretch** - 30 sec each
4. **Calf stretch** - 30 sec each
5. **Hip flexor stretch** - 30 sec each

## Key Points
- Never stretch cold muscles
- Hold stretches without bouncing
- Breathe deeply throughout
- Mild discomfort OK, pain is not
- Focus on problem areas longer

## Additional Recovery
- Foam rolling after stretching
- Hydrate immediately post-run
- Consider ice bath for intense sessions
- Elevate legs for 5-10 minutes

## Frequency
- After every run (at least quick version)
- Full version after hard sessions
- Daily stretching improves results
- More important as weekly mileage increases

## Special Considerations
- Prioritize areas that feel tight
- Add spine twists if back is stiff
- Include IT band stretches if prone to IT issues
- Consider adding meditation/breathing at end
